1. Desire to Acquire: The drive to accumulate different collect and possess things, whether tangible or intangible. 
2. Desire to Bond: The need to be loved and valued in relationships with others. 
3. Desire to Learn: The pursuit of knowledge and understanding, driven by curiosity. 
4. Desire to Defend: The instinct to protect oneself, one's loved ones, and one's property. 
5. Desire to Feel: The longing for emotional experiences, both pleasant and exciting. 
6. Need to survive, self-preservation instinct that triggers when danger to own life or health is percieved, the more the danger - the stronger it becomes. Contributes to itself and F.F.F.F. circuit. Impairs every other desire.
7. Need for food, hunger. It triggers when body requires sustanence, the longer body starved the stronger it becomes to the point of former comrades killing each other for last food. Impairs Empathy, Compassion, Desire to Acquire and Desire to Learn.
8. Need for water, thirst. The strongest of all needs, any living organism requires water to survive, when denied for long enough it easily overrides any other needs and desires. Impairs everything else.
9. Need for sex, lust. The second strongest need, dangerous because it has very high effect even on low activation levels, easily overriding less activated desires and needs. On high activation it can compete with thirst. Enhances Desire to Bond and Desire to Feel.
10. Empathy (the ability to understand and share the feelings of another, both from intellectual point (grasp what they feel) and emotional point (feel what they feel)). Includes the conscious and deliberate effort to understand the thoughts and feelings of another person by imagining oneself in their situation. Enhances Lust.
11. Compassion: An emotional response to another person's suffering or need that is coupled with a genuine desire to help. It is often distinguished from empathy, which is simply sensing and understanding another's feelings. Compassion is what motivates the desire to act. Enhances Empathy.
12. Emotional contagion: The tendency to "catch" or be influenced by the emotions of others. It is an automatic, non-conscious process where observing another's emotional state, often through their expressions or movements, triggers a similar emotional experience in oneself. Enhances Compassion.  
14. Reward and motivation: The brain's circuitry that drives behavior through pleasure-based rewards. This includes the anticipation of rewards, which motivates goal-directed behavior, and the experience of pleasure from obtaining them, which reinforces that behavior. Contributes to itself and all other circuits.

Fight, flight, freeze, and fawn (f.f.f.f.) are automatic, instinctive responses to perceived threats that activate the autonomic nervous system to ensure survival. balance between them is highly individual - some would never freeze and will always fight the threat, others would freeze or flee. But usually each of the four is present in some degree. The four responses, write only one of them:
15.1. Fight: This response prepares the body to confront the threat. It's an aggressive, assertive reaction to a perceived danger, like arguing or becoming physically confrontational. Impairs Empathy and Compassion. 
15.2. Flight: This instinct is to run away from the perceived danger. It's a desire to escape the situation or distance oneself from the threat. Impairs Compassion. 
15.3. Freeze: This response causes temporary paralysis or immobility in the face of danger. It's a state where you feel stuck, unable to move, or even detached from your surroundings. Impares Empathy. 
15.4. Fawn: This is a trauma response involving appeasing the source of the threat to avoid conflict or harm. It can look like people-pleasing, putting others' needs before your own, or constantly trying to fix or rescue others to keep the peace. Impairs Desire to Bond and Desire to Feel. 
16. Pain: The unpleasant sensory and emotional experience associated with actual or potential tissue damage. It is a complex experience that involves both physical sensations and emotional responses, and it can be acute (short-term) or chronic (long-term). Pain serves as a protective mechanism, alerting the body to injury or harm and prompting actions to avoid further damage. Impairs everything else.
17. Pleasure: The positive sensory and emotional experience associated with enjoyable activities or stimuli. It is a complex experience that involves both physical sensations and emotional responses, and it can be derived from various sources, such as food, social interactions, sexual activity, and recreational activities. Pleasure serves as a motivator for behavior, encouraging individuals to seek out experiences that bring them joy and satisfaction. Enhances every desire and need for sex.
